ABOUT SUNFINDER

Sunlight, block by block.

Sunfinder’s Python service calculates the sun direction for Helsinki, fetches building footprints, and projects each estimated building shadow. Your browser renders the interactive map.

In live mode, cloud cover softens or hides the projected shadows. The beta direct sun estimate is one city level hint. Buildings and trees are handled separately by the map. Pick another time and the map shows clear sky potential instead. Building footprints come from OpenStreetMap when available. Use this to find a nice spot in the sun, not as a survey or safety tool.

DIRECT SUN ESTIMATE · BETA

What this number is saying

It is a quick guess about one thing. Can direct sun reach an open spot in Helsinki during the next hour? It looks at now, +30 minutes, and +60 minutes, then averages those three guesses.

The weather comes from one forecast point in central Helsinki. It cannot tell you that every neighbourhood is sunny. It also cannot see a tree, a nearby building, or a small local cloud over your street.

See a small worked example

Imagine a late July day with 60% total cloud, 40% low cloud, 20% rain chance, no rain code, a 35° high sun, and a direct radiation signal of 0.251. The season values are about -0.278 and -0.961. The model adds those clues into a score of 3.57.

chance = 1 / (1 + exp(-3.57)) = 0.97

So it would show about a 97% chance before the extra fog, rain, and heavy cloud checks.

What the range means

This is a Bayesian model now. It keeps a range of believable weights for each clue, not just one fixed weight.

The live range covers 90% of the model’s posterior guesses. It is a range about the model, not a full weather confidence range. It cannot include every small local cloud or tree.

The building shadows on the map are worked out separately from the sun angle and building shape. This estimate uses cloud, rain chance, radiation, sun height, and the time of year. It does not use a camera or a sunshine sensor.
