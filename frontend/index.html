ABOUT SUNFINDER

Sunlight, block by block.

Sunfinder’s Python service supplies the sun angle, sky estimate, and direct sun nowcast. Compact building tiles load in the browser, and the browser projects their shadows while you move through time.

In live mode, cloud cover tells you how likely direct shadows are. The map starts with clear-sky potential, so it still shows where sunlight could reach if clouds open. Turn it off to make shadows follow the sky estimate. The beta direct sun estimate is one city level hint. Buildings and trees are handled separately by the map. Building tiles are based on OpenStreetMap data. Use this to find a nice spot in the sun, not as a survey or safety tool.

DIRECT SUN ESTIMATE · BETA

What this number is saying

It is a quick guess about one thing. Can direct sun reach an open spot in Helsinki during the next hour? It looks at now, +30 minutes, and +60 minutes, then averages those three guesses.

The weather comes from one forecast point in central Helsinki. It cannot tell you that every neighbourhood is sunny. It also cannot see a tree, a nearby building, or a small local cloud over your street.

See a small worked example

Imagine a late July day with 60% total cloud, 40% low cloud, 20% rain chance, no rain code, a 35° high sun, and a direct radiation signal of 0.251. The season values are about -0.278 and -0.961. The MAP score is 3.5729.

MAP chance = 1 / (1 + exp(-3.5729)) = 0.973

That is the ordinary logistic part. The Bayesian part also keeps the uncertainty in the learned weights. For these inputs, it gives a score distribution instead of only one score.

weights | data ≈ Normal(MAP weights, Σ) xᵀΣx = 0.0664, so z | data ≈ Normal(3.5729, 0.2577²) 90% chance range = sigmoid(3.5729 ± 1.645 × 0.2577) = 0.959 to 0.982

So it shows about a 97% chance, with a 96% to 98% Bayesian model range before the extra fog, rain, and heavy cloud checks.

What the range means

This is a Bayesian model now. It keeps a range of believable weights for each clue, not just one fixed weight.

The live range covers 90% of the model’s posterior possibilities. It is a range about the model, not a full weather confidence range. It cannot include every small local cloud or tree.

The building shadows on the map are worked out separately from the sun angle and building shape. This estimate uses cloud, rain chance, radiation, sun height, and the time of year. It does not use a camera or a sunshine sensor.
